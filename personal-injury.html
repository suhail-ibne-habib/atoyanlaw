How Do I Know If I Have a Personal Injury Case?

Determining if you have a personal injury case can be complex. A Los Angeles personal injury lawyer can help, but here are key factors to consider:

  • Was There Negligence? If your injury was caused by someone elseâ€™s careless or reckless actions, you may have a case. Negligence is often a crucial element.
  • Extent of Injuries. The severity of your injuries matters. Cases involving significant medical expenses, long-term treatment, or permanent harm are more likely to qualify.
  • Impact on Your Life. Consider how the injury has affected your daily life, ability to work, and overall well-being.
  • Evidence to Support Your Claim. Cases with clear evidence, like witness statements, photos, or reports, are stronger and easier to pursue.

What Does A Personal Injury Lawyer Handle?

An injury lawyer plays a critical role in handling injury claims. Their responsibilities include the following:

  • Handling Documentation: Preparing and filing legal documents, ensuring compliance with deadlines and procedural requirements.
  • Representing Clients in Court: If a settlement cannot be reached, the lawyer takes the case to trial to fight for fair compensation.

Compensation in California Personal Injury Cases

Your compensation in a personal injury case typically covers various damages. This may include, but is not limited to, medical expenses for treatment, rehabilitation, and ongoing care related to the injury; loss of income if you've been unable to work; loss of future earning capacity if the injury impacts your ability to work in the future; pain and suffering; emotional distress; and property damage to your vehicle or personal belongings.

Punitive damages may also be awarded to punish the negligent party for particularly egregious actions. 

Personal injury compensation aims to restore you to your original physical and financial health condition. While this may not be possible where permanent disability has occurred, settlement is aimed at giving you the financial resources required for future injury-related needs. 

Comparative Negligence in California Personal Injury Law

California operates under a pure â€œcomparative faultâ€ rule in personal injury cases. Under this rule, you can still recover damages even if you are found to be partially at fault for the accident. However, your total compensation will be reduced by the percentage of your responsibility. 

For instance, if you are found by the court to be 20% at fault for an accident, your compensation will be reduced by 20%. This doctrine underscores the importance of having a skilled personal injury attorney to help you fight for maximum compensation.
